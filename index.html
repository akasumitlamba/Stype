New version

A faster way to turn speech into usable text.

Open Stype, press your hotkey, speak naturally, and get clean text pasted into the app you were already using. The dashboard stays available for setup, while the small pill overlay handles the actual dictation flow.

This release adds better control over the local engine, audio input, hotkeys, system sound capture, personal vocabulary, replacements, and transcription history.

01 - Engine

Pick speed, accuracy, and hardware.

The Engine tab exposes Fast, Balanced, Accurate, and Best model modes, plus CPU or NVIDIA CUDA processing. Applying a change reloads the engine in-app.

02 - Audio

Control the microphone, hotkey, and system sound.

Choose a microphone, capture a global shortcut, or switch the hotkey to transcribe audio playing from Windows with the new speaker control on the pill.

03 - Dictionary

Teach Stype the words you actually use.

Add vocabulary terms or replacement pairs. Stype feeds those entries into the transcription prompt and applies post-processing so phrases like "out words" can become "outwards".

04 - History

Find, copy, export, or clear transcriptions.

Every transcription is stored locally with time, word count, and character count. The History tab adds search, one-click copy, import, export, and clear-all actions.

How it works

Press, speak, paste, keep moving.

1

Launch Stype

The tray icon starts, the local Whisper model loads, and the dashboard can stay hidden in the background.

2

Tap your hotkey

The pill opens the selected input, switches to Listening, and displays blocky equalizer bars from the audio level.

3

Finish or switch source

Press the hotkey again, click the cancel area if needed, or use the left speaker button for system sound capture.

4

Text appears

Stype formats the transcript, stores it in local history, copies it, and sends Ctrl + V into the active app.

Private by default

No account. No subscriptions. No cloud audio.

Stype runs the model locally, stores settings locally, and keeps your dictated text on your Windows machine.
